experience

most of my experience so far has been in roles where i’ve had to think quickly, communicate clearly, and deal with people in real time. even when the work wasn’t “creative” on paper, it still shaped how i think about tone, presentation, and the way people experience things.

university of guelph
student desk clerk · 2022 – present

this is a front-facing role where i handle questions, issues, and unexpected situations from students. it can get busy quickly, so a lot of it is about staying calm, communicating clearly, and figuring out what needs to happen next.

it’s probably the role that’s shaped me the most. i’ve gotten a lot better at reading situations, adjusting how i communicate, and helping people feel understood even when things aren’t going smoothly.

law society of ontario
summer student, trustee services · 2025

i worked on administrative and operational tasks in a structured, confidential environment. a lot of the work involved organizing documents, tracking information, and making sure things were accurate and easy to follow.

what i took away from this role was how important clarity and structure are, especially when the stakes are high. it made me more detail-oriented and more aware of how information is presented and used by other people.

what i’ve been taking from all of this

across different roles, i’ve noticed i care a lot about how things come across to people — whether that’s through communication, structure, or visuals.

that’s part of why i’ve been getting more interested in creative and content work. i like thinking about presentation, tone, and the details that make something feel intentional.
