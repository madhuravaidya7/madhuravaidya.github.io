experience

so far

Most of my experience has been in environments where working directly with people is central to the role.

I’ve spent time in student-facing and administrative settings, supporting people in shared spaces and responding to questions or issues as they come up. This kind of work made me comfortable adjusting how I communicate depending on context, pace, and the person in front of me.

I’ve also worked in more structured, institutional environments, where accuracy, discretion, and clear communication mattered. Across these settings, I’ve gotten used to reading situations quickly and helping things run smoothly when there’s a lot happening at once.

right now

I’m currently finishing my BSc in neuroscience, which shapes how I think about digital culture and communication.

I’m especially interested in attention, emotion, and decision-making, and how those processes show up in online spaces. I tend to notice how people interact with content, what holds attention, what gets ignored, and how tone or timing changes the way something is received.

Outside of school and work, I keep up closely with pop culture and current events, and I spend a lot of time around collective, fast-moving spaces. Playing music, practicing taekwondo, and following baseball, soccer, and hockey have all influenced how I think about group dynamics, energy, and shared norms.
